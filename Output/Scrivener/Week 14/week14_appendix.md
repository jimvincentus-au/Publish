# Week 14 Appendix: Citizenship and Knowledge Under Command

This was an exceptionally heavy week for structural democratic erosion, with the executive launching coordinated attacks on multiple guardrails while courts and civil society mounted notable, but reactive, resistance. The most acute pressures fell on rule-of-law and separation-of-powers traits: open defiance of Supreme Court orders on deportations, contempt findings, and the arrest of a sitting judge for alleged obstruction of ICE all signal an executive willing to treat law as optional and courts as adversaries. Simultaneously, the administration weaponized federal power against perceived enemies—elite universities, sanctuary jurisdictions, ActBlue, and immigrant communities—while hollowing out oversight (closure of detention watchdog offices, politicization of Interior and State, DOGE opacity). Civil and equality protections were sharply downgraded via executive orders dismantling disparate-impact enforcement, DEI-linked research, and disability services, and through aggressive immigration enforcement that swept up green-card holders and even citizens. Foreign and security policy showed chaotic, personalized decision-making: a Russia-friendly Ukraine “peace” plan, tariff chaos, and a Pentagon in disarray under Hegseth. Countervailing forces—mass protests, multiple injunctions, Harvard and state AG lawsuits, restoration of some protections—demonstrate institutional resilience but do not offset the week’s net authoritarian drift.
