The week was dominated by Operation Epic Fury, a large-scale U.S.–Israeli assault on Iran launched by President Trump without congressional authorization. The strikes killed Iran’s supreme leader, triggered regional missile retaliation, disrupted the Strait of Hormuz, and produced mounting civilian and U.S. military casualties, even as the administration struggled to define whether the U.S. is formally at war. Congress debated but ultimately rejected war powers resolutions, leaving the operation largely unchecked while the State Department scrambled, belatedly, to warn and assist hundreds of thousands of Americans stranded across the Middle East.
