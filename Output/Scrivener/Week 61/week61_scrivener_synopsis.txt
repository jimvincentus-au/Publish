Across Week 61, the Democracy Clock did not move, but the grooves of erosion deepened. Trump expanded the Iran war through unilateral bombardment and “no quarter” rhetoric, invoked emergency powers to reopen a spill‑prone pipeline, and claimed an absolute right to set tariffs and withdraw from NATO. Congress rejected a war‑powers limit, stumbled over DHS funding, and held no serious hearings on costs or strategy. Intelligence leaders trimmed their testimony to fit the White House line, while FBI purges, warrantless data purchases, and DHS AI projects widened surveillance. In immigration, expanded 287(g) policing, wrongful deportations, deaths in custody, and harsh charges against protesters turned enforcement into a laboratory for stratified citizenship. Simultaneously, the SAVE Act, state citizenship challenges, and DOJ voter‑roll suits narrowed access to the ballot under “integrity” branding. FCC license threats, curated Epstein disclosures, and politicized advisory bodies tightened control over information. Courts blocked some excesses, but mainly as embattled islands in a thickening web of unchecked authority.
