Week 57 Appendix: Immigration as Testing Ground
This was a structurally heavy week for democratic erosion, centered on three converging fronts: immigration authoritarianism, institutional capture, and information control. The administration deepened a mass-detention, mass-deportation model—expanding ICE’s budget, tech stack, and local deputization while openly violating court orders and a Minnesota refugee injunction. DHS and DOJ were repeatedly used as political weapons against disfavored states and communities, even as judges in several cases (tariffs, deportations, historic preservation, immigrant bond rights) pushed back, underscoring a widening executive–judicial confrontation. Economic and regulatory policy showed classic crony capitalism: glyphosate and climate rollbacks, a luxury DHS jet, and opaque Trump-family business deals, alongside a $10 billion transfer to the “Board of Peace” and an attempted parallel foreign-policy architecture. Simultaneously, the FCC’s equal-time reinterpretation, CBS self-censorship, and selective investigations into critical outlets signaled a coordinated effort to chill oppositional media. On public health, RFK Jr. and Bhattacharya’s moves at HHS/CDC and ACIP point toward dismantling evidence-based vaccination infrastructure. Countervailing forces—state and local resistance to ICE, civil-rights litigation, impeachment articles against Noem, and the Supreme Court’s tariff ruling—were significant but outweighed by the breadth and entrenchment of authoritarian-style practices.
