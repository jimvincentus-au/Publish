Week 60 Appendix: Consolidation by War and Ballot
This was an acute-rupture week in which an already aggressive war in Iran, a sweeping healthcare rollback, and a frontal assault on voting access converged into a coherent authoritarian pattern. Congress largely failed to constrain the executive on war powers or military spending, while the administration normalized emergency authorities to bypass both budgetary and foreign-policy oversight. The House passage of the Save America Act, coupled with Trump’s veto threats to force its enactment, represents a major structural push toward mass voter disenfranchisement, especially of women, immigrants, and minorities. Simultaneously, DHS oversight offices were gutted, ICE and DOJ were weaponized against targeted communities, and anti-terror statutes were used against left-wing protesters, deepening the fusion of security policy with partisan goals. On the economic and regulatory front, the One Big Beautiful Bill Act and environmental rollbacks shifted risk and suffering onto the poor and sick while war-driven energy decisions enriched strategic partners like Russia. Media and information controls tightened around the Iran war, with propaganda, access restrictions, and casualty obfuscation undermining transparency. Courts provided some countervailing wins, but they were outweighed by structural entrenchment of executive impunity, crony capitalism, and stratified citizenship.
