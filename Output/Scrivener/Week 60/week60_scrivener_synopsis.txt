Week 60 of Trump’s second term was defined less by rupture than by consolidation. The Democracy Clock held at 8:13 p.m., yet dozens of traits of democratic erosion deepened in concert. Operation Epic Fury expanded into a regional Iran war, with civilian casualties, underreported troop losses, and economic shocks around the Strait of Hormuz. Congress blocked war-powers limits and accepted emergency funding maneuvers, effectively normalizing executive war-making. At home, House Republicans advanced the SAVE/Save America Act, tying all legislation to a bill that would sharply restrict voting access, while Utah officials moved to blunt anti-gerrymandering reforms and reshape their supreme court. Security and immigration agencies gutted internal oversight and targeted immigrants, protesters, and Muslim communities under terrorism rhetoric. Simultaneously, health and environmental protections were rolled back, regulatory agencies courted corporate litigants, and billionaires and foreign-linked insiders steered tax, sanctions, and arms policies. Information about war and elite misconduct was tightly curated, even as courts offered scattered pockets of resistance.
