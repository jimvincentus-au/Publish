Title
-----
War Without Authorization, Law Under Strain

Subtitle
--------
A unilateral bombing campaign, troops in American streets, and narrowed judicial remedies reveal a system where law still exists but binds power unevenly.

Tagline / Hook
-------------
At 10:58 p.m., the explosions are new but the architecture is not: power moves freely, and the law arrives late, in smaller pieces.

Synopses
--------
Long Synopsis:
Week 23 unfolds as a study in unilateral power that does not move the Democracy Clock because the hour already assumed it. President Trump orders large‑scale airstrikes on Iranian nuclear sites without congressional authorization, sidelining the War Powers Resolution and treating intelligence as malleable. The conflict abroad becomes justification for deploying marines and National Guard troops into Los Angeles to back sweeping, often lawless immigration raids that ensnare citizens, long‑time residents, and elected officials. Courts push back in individual cases, freeing detainees, blocking some deportations, and restraining budgetary retaliation, yet the Supreme Court simultaneously narrows nationwide injunctions and trims rights protections, hardening executive advantage. Public health and information systems are politicized through purges at CDC panels, deep cuts to cancer research, and the dismantling of Voice of America. Community organizing, local elections, and scattered judicial victories show resilience, but the week’s events knit together a polity where law persists while its capacity to constrain power steadily thins.

Short Synopsis:
Unilateral war, domestic troop deployments, and aggressive immigration raids expose an executive already operating beyond robust oversight, while Supreme Court rulings and politicized science narrow the remaining tools to check it.

Actionable Outcomes
-------------------
- Support nonpartisan war‑powers and civil‑military oversight groups that track and challenge unauthorized uses of force and domestic troop deployments.
- Donate to or volunteer with immigrant legal defense networks and community bail funds that respond to mass raids and wrongful detentions.
- Back independent investigative journalism and local newsrooms that scrutinize intelligence claims, detention conditions, and politicized prosecutions.
- Engage with civil liberties and voting‑rights organizations working to defend birthright citizenship, due process, and protections against discriminatory immigration policy.
- Advocate at the state and local level for transparency ordinances on law‑enforcement cooperation with federal agencies and for limits on military‑style policing.
- Support public‑interest science and health organizations that defend evidence‑based advisory panels and resist politicization of vaccine and research policy.
