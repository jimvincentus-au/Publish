Title
-----
Crony Capitalism as Operating System

Subtitle
--------
A twenty‑minute leap on the Democracy Clock as civil service, law, and public wealth are rewired to serve loyalty, profit, and control.

Tagline / Hook
-------------
This week the machinery of government did not merely misfire; it was rewired so that public offices, data, and law increasingly serve a narrow circle of insiders.

Synopses
--------
Long Synopsis:
Week 7 of Trump’s second term marks a sharp consolidation of power rather than a single spectacular break. The Democracy Clock jumps from 9:12 p.m. to 9:32 p.m. as civil service protections, regulatory agencies, and financial rules are systematically repurposed. DOGE‑driven mass firings at Social Security, Veterans Affairs, the IRS, State, and Education hollow out neutral capacity and invite partisan and private replacements. Law is openly bent toward friends and against enemies: a disfavored law firm is targeted by executive order, prosecutions of well‑connected crypto figures stall, and AI‑driven visa revocations punish ideology. A Strategic Bitcoin Reserve and digital asset stockpile blur public policy with insider portfolios, while tariffs and trade whiplash offload risk onto the public. Universities, immigrants, and protesters face financial and legal pressure to conform. Courts and civil society still resist in places—blocking some firings, funding freezes, and anti‑labor moves—but the week’s net effect is to thicken the walls around power and narrow the channels of accountability.

Short Synopsis:
Civil service purges, agency capture, and a crypto‑infused economic agenda fuse loyalty, profit, and state power, even as courts and protesters mount partial resistance. The Democracy Clock jumps twenty minutes forward.

Actionable Outcomes
-------------------
- Support nonpartisan watchdogs and legal defense funds that protect civil servants, whistleblowers, and inspectors general facing politicized firings or retaliation.
- Back independent investigative journalism and data transparency projects that track agency cuts, crypto policy, and manipulation of economic or education statistics.
- Engage with local school boards and university governance to defend academic freedom, inclusive curricula, and resistance to politically driven book bans or funding threats.
- Participate in or donate to organizations providing legal aid to immigrants and targeted communities facing AI‑driven surveillance, mass deportations, or stratified access to rights.
- Advocate with members of Congress and state legislators for strong anti‑corruption, conflict‑of‑interest, and campaign finance rules that limit pay‑to‑play policymaking.
- Join or organize civic education efforts—public forums, reading groups, teach‑ins—that explain how civil service protections, appropriations, and oversight are supposed to work.
