    • The Six Promises of American Democracy

      A modern restatement of the Preamble’s commitments: justice, tranquility, defense, welfare, liberty, posterity. Shows how every reform and every deliverable in the book flows from these six promises. Anchors the reader in the founding purpose of the republic—not nostalgia, but obligation.

    • Why This Moment Matters

      A clear explanation of the stakes of the 2026 midterms. Shows how democracies break and how they recover. Describes the conditions left after Year One of Trump’s second term and why 2026 will decide whether the country drifts toward permanent minority rule or restores democratic self-government.

    • Five Phases of Democratic Repair

      The strategic arc that frames the entire book: Defense, Resistance, Restoration, Redemption, Reinstitution. Shows where we have been (Defense/Resistance), where we stand (early Restoration), and what is required to finish the journey (Redemption & Reinstitution). This is the roadmap.

    • Imperatives of a Modern Democracy

      Consent, Constraint, Function, Trust — plus Shared Reality and Future Preparedness. A clear explanation of what democratic machinery must do to remain legitimate. These imperatives organize and justify the structural reforms that follow.

    • Consent: Elections That Count Every Voice

      A unified chapter covering campaign finance reform, voting access, Electoral College modernization, gerrymandering, election certification, and public trust in elections. Demonstrates that true consent requires an unrigged system.

    • Constraint: No One Above the Law

      Reforms ensuring executive power cannot be abused and oversight cannot be neutered. Includes limits on emergency powers, strengthened inspectors general, guardrails on pardons, and restored congressional authority. Shows how to prevent another Trump—or anyone—from erasing accountability.

    • Function: Courts and Institutions That Actually Work

      Judicial reform (ethics, term limits, expansion), FOIA modernization, agency depoliticization, and anti-corruption infrastructure. Shows how to rebuild institutions that serve law, not loyalty.

    • Trust: Information, Platforms, and Civic Literacy

      Platform transparency, disinformation safeguards, broadcast fairness, and civic education. Demonstrates the link between information integrity and democratic legitimacy. Closes with the idea that a shared reality is a public good.

    • A Nation That Cares for Its Own

      Health care, housing, childcare, food security, fair taxation. Shows that democratic legitimacy depends on a basic threshold of care. Ties each deliverable to daily lived experience.

    • A Nation That Educates and Elevates

      K–12 reform, higher education affordability, worker power, economic mobility. Shows the relationship between education, dignity, and equal opportunity.

    • A Nation That Is Safe, Just, and Free

      Policing reform, gun safety, criminal justice reform, infrastructure, cybersecurity, protection against foreign interference. Emphasizes safety without authoritarianism.

    • A Nation That Belongs to Everyone

      Immigration, belonging, equal treatment, digital safety, environmental justice. Shows how shared citizenship and equal dignity are the heart of self-government.

    • A Nation That Prepares for the Future

      Climate resilience, technology readiness, public service, civic re-engagement, and long-term investment. Shows that democracies that last prepare—not drift.
